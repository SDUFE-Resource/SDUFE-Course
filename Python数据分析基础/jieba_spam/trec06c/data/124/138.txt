About GE
 
GE (NYSE: GE) is Imagination at Work -- a diversified technology, media and financial services company focused on solving some of the world\'s toughest problems. With products and services ranging from aircraft engines, power generation, water processing and security technology to medical imaging, business and consumer financing, media content and advanced materials, GE serves customers in more than 100 countries and employs more than 300,000 people worldwide.  Jeffrey R. Immelt is Chairman of the Board and Chief Executive Officer of GE. 
 
GE\'s revenue reached $152.4billion in 2004.
 
The company traces its beginnings to Thomas A. Edison, who established Edison Electric Light Company in 1878. In 1892, a merger of Edison General Electric Company and Thomson-Houston Electric Company created General Electric Company. GE is the only company listed in the Dow Jones Industrial Index today that was also included in the original index in 1896.
 
GE business divisions
Commercial Finance, Consumer Financial Services, Infrastructure, Industrial, Healthcare, NBC-Universal
 
GE in China
GE started doing business in China as early as 1906 and was considered one of the most active foreign companies in the country at the time.  In 1908, the first GE light plant was built in Shenyang.  GE acquired Anderson, Meyer &amp; Co. in 1934 and started to provide installation and repair services of imported 
electric equipment in China.  GE resumed trade with People\'s Republic of China in 1979.  It opened GE Hangwei Medical Systems Co., Ltd in Beijing, its first joint venture in China in 1991.  
All of GE\'s industrial businesses have set up operations in China with about  11,000 employees. GE has established over 40 legal entities in China with total investment over US$1.5 billion.  Its financial services sector is seeking business opportunities as WTO opens China market.  
The sales revenue of GE in China is $3.9 billion in 2004.   
Besides business commitment, GE continues to act as a good corporate citizen in China, supporting Chinese educational institutions with scholarship programs and contributing to the communities where it operates.  Elfun, the GE volunteer organization with chapters in Hong Kong, Beijing, Shanghai Guangzhou, Dalian and Hong Kong, creates and participates in such activities as community services and environmental protection.
